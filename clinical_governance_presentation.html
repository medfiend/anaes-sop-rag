02 / Regulatory Exemption

Clinical Safety & SaMD Qualification

Bypassing Software as a Medical Device (SaMD) Exemption:

  • Dynamic drug calculators that evaluate patient parameters trigger strict MHRA device classifications (Class IIa/b), introducing severe legal liability.
  • Mitigation Pathway: AnaesSOP implements a pre-calculated Tape-Scroller Lookup List at 1kg intervals, simulating a passive reference booklet.
  • Adjacent weight rows remain visible to verify calculation trends and instantly expose database typos, satisfying MHRA lookup verifiability rules.
Control Measure: Complies with NHS DCB0129 Clinical Safety Standards. Fully documented in the safety case file.

Technical Security & Data Governance

AnaesSOP Governance Business Case

Clinical Safety, Technical Security, and Departmental Pilot Roadmap

1. Executive Summary

In acute clinical settings (Anaesthetics, Intensive Care, Emergency Medicine), rapid access to Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) and emergency calculators directly impacts patient outcomes. The current infrastructure relies on desktop-bound intranet portals or physical binders, both of which introduce cognitive load, search latency, and security issues.

AnaesSOP is a secure, offline-first, mobile-friendly clinical guidelines retrieval system. Grounded strictly in verified departmental documents (hallucination-free RAG), it reduces guideline retrieval speeds from minutes to under three seconds, supporting clinicians at the point of care.

2. Clinical Safety & SaMD Regulatory Qualification

Under the UK MHRA stand-alone software guidelines and the EU Medical Device Regulation (MDR 2017/745), any software tool that accepts patient-specific parameters (such as weight) and performs dynamic calculations to determine drug dosages is classified as a regulated Medical Device (SaMD).

To protect the Trust from regulatory liability, AnaesSOP uses a passive reference lookup scroller:

  • Compile-Time Precalculation: Dynamic calculation functions are removed. Calculations are precalculated for every 1kg step (e.g. 10kg to 200kg) during compilation.
  • Tape-Scroller Interface: Clinicians scroll a vertical list to highlight their target weight.
  • Adjacent Context: Neighbouring weights and dosage outputs remain fully visible. This conforms to MHRA rules on user-verifiable lookups, allowing clinicians to instantly audit mathematical trends and catch typos.
  • Grounded Verification: Every output links directly to the corresponding PDF guideline page.
NHS DCB0129 Clinical Safety Standards
A complete safety case and hazard log have been drafted to reduce clinical risks to "As Low As Reasonably Practicable" (ALARP). See the safety case file.

3. Technical Security & Data Governance (NHS DTAC Alignment)

To align with the Digital Technology Assessment Criteria (DTAC) requirements, the backend and Cloudflare Edge Worker routing have been hardened:

  • Cryptographic Edge Validation: Enforces non-bypassable Clerk JWT signature validation using SubtleCrypto Web APIs on the worker's `/ingest` route, blocking unauthorized uploads.
  • Single-File Constraints: R2 buckets are locked down. Input validation on `file` and `id` parameters blocks directory traversal (`..`, `/`, `\`) and key listing wildcards (`*`, `?`), preventing bucket enumeration attacks.
  • Secure Headers: Enforces HSTS (`max-age=31536000; includeSubDomains; preload`), `X-Content-Type-Options: nosniff`, and a strict Content-Security-Policy (CSP) across routing logic.
  • Zero PII Processing: The database only hosts public clinical guidelines and phone directories. No patient identifiable information (PII) is processed or stored.

4. Pilot Roadmap (8-Week Implementation)

A controlled rollout is proposed to evaluate usability and verify the Mean Time to Information Retrieval (MTIR) reduction before hospital-wide deployment:

Phase Timeline Milestones & Deliverables
1. Scope & Ingestion Weeks 1 - 2 Select 10-15 pilot guidelines; compile metadata; map site contact extensions.
2. Staging Sandbox Weeks 3 - 4 Deploy staging edge nodes; connect Clerk authentication; test token forwarding.
3. Departmental Pilot Weeks 5 - 8 Deploy Progressive Web App (PWA) to 20-30 clinicians; log bedside usability and MTIR telemetry.
4. Evaluation Week 9+ Analyze audit logs and present performance statistics to the Clinical Audit Lead.
